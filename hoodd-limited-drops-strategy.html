LIMITED EDITION DROP PROGRAM

Hood'd Shop · Pre-Tournament & World Cup 2026 Drop Strategy · Built 2026-04-15

Strategic Framing

The limited-edition drop program exists to (1) differentiate Hood'd from Amazon generic hood covers at the $15–28 price band, (2) generate a structured content engine that fills the 13-week pre-tournament window with organic-shareable moments, and (3) build a creator-driven distribution channel that compounds before paid ads are switched on. The program runs from April 28, 2026 through the World Cup Final on July 19, 2026, then sunsets.

Co-designed with influencers, athletes, or cultural figures. Creator drives audience, we execute production + fulfillment. Split rev share creates alignment. Marketing volume far exceeds house drops because the creator owns promotion.

Price: $64.99–$69.99

Core Principles (Non-Negotiable)

  1. Scarcity is time-boxed, not quantity-boxed. POD means no inventory. Credibility comes from a countdown window, a dated archive, and an auto-unpublish that actually fires.
  2. Limited means visually different. Not a recolor, not a badge swap. A limited design is a new art direction. No exceptions.
  3. Never discount a limited edition. Limiteds price above the standard line. Any discounting undermines the premium positioning and trains the audience to wait.
  4. Every drop has a written IP agreement before creative starts. No exceptions. No "we'll sort it later."
  5. Every drop generates content for the library. Minimum: 1 hero photo, 1 short-form video, 1 campaign caption, 1 post-drop receipt graphic.
  6. Archive everything. Every closed drop lives on a public "Past Drops" page as proof that the windows actually close.

Interpretation
Base case contributes ~$62K, which is material but not the whole business — the standard line does the heavier revenue lifting during the tournament. What We Must Measure to Validate

  • First-drop CAC — attributed spend ÷ new customers acquired through drops
  • Second purchase rate — % of drop buyers who return for any product within 21 days

Celebrity / Creator Collab Program

The creator program exists to import trust, distribution, and authenticity that Hood'd can't manufacture in-house. The economic model splits the premium above the standard line: creator gets $7/unit, Hood'd captures the design-driven pricing lift plus new-customer acquisition.

Three Tier Framework

Tier A Aspirational

Audience: 1M+ followers, cultural figures, current or recent players, major YouTubers.

Reality check: At Hood'd's current stage, Tier A is unlikely to engage on a revenue-share model. Their endorsement rates are $25K–$500K+ per post minimum.

Strategy: Plant seeds now (gift product, tag, congratulate publicly), do not send formal outreach until we have $100K+ in monthly revenue and a proven drop track record. Revisit July.

Examples: Retired-but-active-on-social ex-players, major football YouTube channels, mainstream lifestyle creators.

Tier B Primary focus

Audience: 100K–1M followers in football culture, car culture, diaspora communities, regional football scenes.

Deal structure: Co-design + $7/unit rev share + free units for their team. No upfront fee unless proven track record.

Realistic target: 6–10 collabs across the program. At 75 units avg per drop, creator earns $525/drop.

Archetypes to recruit: Mid-tier football YouTubers/TikTokers, ex-professional players now creators, regional football TikTokers (Mexican, Argentine, Brazilian, Moroccan, Nigerian diaspora), car/automotive creators with football crossover.

Tier C Volume seeding

Audience: 10K–100K engaged followers, niche authority.

Deal structure: Free product seeding + discount code with attribution tracking + $5/unit rev share on code usage. Co-design only for top performers.

Realistic target: 20–30 creators across the program for seeding. 3–5 get promoted to a formal co-design drop.

Archetypes: Amateur football creators, weekend-car enthusiasts, niche nation-focused accounts, college football supporters, local supporter groups.

Target Profile Filter (Before Outreach)

Before sending any outreach, every candidate must pass these five filters:

  1. Vehicle fit. Do they drive? Is a car visible in recent content? (No car = no credibility with product.)
  2. Engagement rate. Likes + comments + shares on recent 10 posts ÷ follower count. Target ≥3%. A 500K account at 0.5% engagement is a worse partner than a 50K account at 6%.
  3. Nation/cultural alignment. Do they rep a nation authentically? Or would the collab look forced?
  4. Taste. Does their grid, any merch they've released, and their aesthetic suggest they can co-design something we'd actually be proud to ship?
  5. Audience geography. At least 40% of their audience in US/Canada (Hood'd's serviceable market). A 2M follower Mexican creator with 5% US audience underperforms a 200K Mexican-American creator with 80% US audience.

End-to-End Operating Pipeline

Every drop follows this 17-day workflow. Day 0 is first contact, Day 17 is settlement. Shorter cycles are possible for match-moment drops (see Launch Day tab).

1

Identification & Screening · Days 0–3

Creator is identified (inbound, referral, or cold research) and profiled against the five-filter checklist. If they pass, a brief is sent: Hood'd story, collab concept, target nation/theme, deal structure, timeline.

Output: Creator file in Airtable/Notion CRM with profile, filter scores, and outreach status.

2

Contracting · Days 3–5

1-page collab agreement executed via e-sign. Covers: IP ownership (Hood'd), revenue share ($7/unit), minimum deliverables (3 posts: teaser, launch, recap), timeline, drop window, content usage rights for Hood'd, kill clause if creator goes silent.

Output: Signed agreement filed in collabs/[drop-slug]/agreement.pdf.

3

Design Kickoff · Days 5–7

30-min async DM exchange or short call. Creator describes vision: colors, symbols, mood, references. Hood'd captures into a structured brief. Agreed: 3 rounds max, 14-day design window, then collab ends.

Output: Design brief doc.

4

Design Iteration · Days 7–12

Round 1 (Day 7): three directions generated (AI tools + Hood'd design language). Creator picks or combines.
Round 2 (Day 9): refined single direction, creator notes.
Round 3 (Day 11): final. Creator approves in writing (signed approval doc or email reply).

Output: Print-ready files sent to PrintKK; approval archived.

5

Pre-Launch Content & Setup · Days 12–16

Creator films teaser + launch content (30–60 sec each). Hood'd builds the drop product page, configures countdown timer and auto-unpublish, builds the email + push campaign, and sets up attribution tracking.

Output: Drop page staged, content scheduled, email drafted, creator content reviewed and approved.

6

Launch & Drop Window · Days 16–18 (48-hour window)

Drop goes live at a pre-committed time (Tuesday/Thursday 10am ET is the default). Creator posts on schedule. Hood'd amplifies through owned channels and runs a boosted post with a small budget ($100–250). Mid-drop check-in at 24hr mark. See Launch Day tab for the full hour-by-hour runbook.

Output: Drop closes. Variant auto-unpublishes. Landing page converts to "Sold Out" or "Archive" state.

7

Post-Drop Settlement & Retro · Days 18–22

Hood'd reports to creator: units sold, revenue, their cut, timeline for payout. Payment within 14 days. Internal retro: what converted, what didn't, what to change on the next drop. Content archive — all assets logged to content library for ongoing remarketing. Creator drop added to public "Past Drops" page.

Output: Settlement statement, retro notes in CRM, content library updated, archive page live.

Match-Moment Drop Template System

During the tournament, drops are match-driven not calendar-driven. The window to capitalize on a moment is ~36 hours from match end. To hit that window, every realistic trigger must have a pre-designed template ready before kickoff.

Pre-designed template set (48 nations × 1 base template = 48 files):

  • "Group Stage Win" — each nation's standard flag + "GROUP STAGE 2026" overlay
  • "Round of 16 Advance" — each possible advancer, unlocked when they win their group-stage last match
  • "Upset" — generic underdog template adaptable to any nation color palette
  • "Final Four" — pre-designed for each semifinalist slot
  • "Finalist" — pre-designed for each finalist slot
  • "Champion 2026" — pre-designed for each likely champion + generic fallback

Workflow on match day:

  1. Match ends. Decision made within 30 min: does this warrant a drop?
  2. If yes: pull template, customize (swap nation asset, update date, finalize copy) — 2hr work
  3. Upload to PrintKK as new variant — 30 min
  4. Launch drop page with 36-hour window — 1hr
  5. Email + social within 6 hours of match end
Required staffing for match-moment drops
Match-moment drops require someone on-call for every match day. This is currently a one-person operation (Dan). Either automate heavily with templates, or accept only 1–2 match-moment drops per round rather than trying to drop after every match.
