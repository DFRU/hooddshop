## Document Purpose

A collaboration agreement between **Hood'd Shop Inc.** ("Hood'd"), a corporation organized under the laws of Ontario, Canada, and **[Creator Legal Name]** ("Creator") for a single limited-edition product drop. Each drop is its own agreement. Not a master services agreement.

---

## 1. The Collaboration

Creator and Hood'd will collaborate on the design of a single limited-edition car hood cover (the "Drop Product") sold under the Hood'd Shop brand, exclusively on hooddshop.com, for the time-limited window defined in Section 4.

**Drop Slug:** `[e.g., creator-handle-nation-date]`
**Drop Nation / Theme:** `[e.g., Mexico — Día de Muertos edition]`
**Retail Price:** `$[64.99 or 69.99] USD`

## 2. Creator Deliverables

Creator agrees to provide:

1. Initial design input via one design kickoff session (async or live, approximately 30 minutes).
2. Written approval of final design (email or messaging platform acceptable; "final approval" defined in Section 8).
3. Minimum promotional content on Creator's owned channels (Instagram, TikTok, YouTube, and/or X as applicable):
   - **1 teaser post** 3–5 days before drop launch
   - **1 launch post** within 2 hours of drop going live
   - **1 recap post** within 48 hours of drop close
   - Minimum **2 Stories** across the drop window
4. Confidentiality regarding the design, pricing, and launch date until drop goes live.
5. Good-faith participation in design iteration per Section 8 timelines.
6. **Endorsement disclosure compliance.** All promotional content shall comply with the U.S. FTC's Guides Concerning the Use of Endorsements and Testimonials (16 CFR Part 255), the Canadian Competition Act, and any applicable platform rules. Creator shall include clear and conspicuous disclosure (e.g., `#ad`, `#sponsored`, or platform-native paid partnership tag) on all Drop Product content, placed where it will be seen before a consumer engages with the endorsement.

## 3. Hood'd Deliverables

Hood'd agrees to provide:

1. Design production — up to 3 rounds of refinement based on Creator input.
2. Print-ready files delivered to manufacturing partner.
3. Product page build-out, countdown mechanic, and drop infrastructure.
4. Fulfillment from order placement through customer delivery.
5. Customer service for all orders.
6. Two complimentary units of the Drop Product shipped to Creator at Hood'd's cost.
7. Post-drop settlement statement and payment per Section 5.

## 4. Drop Window

- **Drop opens:** `[Date, 10:00am ET]`
- **Drop closes:** 48 hours after opening.
- **Post-drop availability:** None. Product is permanently unavailable on hooddshop.com after window close, except as stockouts, test units, or warranty replacements.

Hood'd reserves the right to adjust opening time by up to 24 hours with 48 hours advance notice to Creator.

## 5. Revenue Share

5.1 **Rate.** Creator shall receive **seven United States dollars (US$7.00) per unit** of the Drop Product sold during the drop window.

5.2 **Sale definition.** A "unit sold" means a completed order as reported by Shopify, net of:
(a) refunds issued within 30 days of drop close; and
(b) chargebacks received within 90 days of drop close that are not successfully disputed by Hood'd.

5.3 **Reconciliation window.** Hood'd shall issue an initial settlement and payment within **fourteen (14) calendar days** after drop close based on completed orders as of that date. A final reconciliation covering refunds and chargebacks shall be issued within **ninety (90) days** after drop close. If the final reconciliation shows an overpayment, Creator agrees to refund the overpaid amount within 30 days of notice, or Hood'd may offset against future drops.

5.4 **Currency and payment method.** All amounts are in United States dollars. Payment will be issued via **Stripe Connect** by default. Alternative methods (PayPal, wire, Wise) available on request; Creator bears any third-party fees exceeding Stripe Connect equivalents.

5.5 **Tax.** Creator is responsible for all taxes on revenue received. Creator shall deliver required tax documentation (W-8BEN for non-U.S. persons, W-9 for U.S. persons, or Canadian equivalent) **before any payment is issued**. No payment will be released until valid tax documentation is on file.

5.6 **Audit right.** Upon no less than 10 business days' written notice and not more than once per twelve-month period, Creator (or an independent accountant bound by confidentiality and engaged at Creator's expense) may inspect Hood'd's records reasonably necessary to verify amounts paid under this agreement. If an audit reveals an underpayment greater than 5% of amounts owed, Hood'd shall pay the underpaid amount plus reasonable audit costs.

## 6. Intellectual Property

6.1 **Work for hire and assignment.** The Drop Product design is a "work made for hire" to the maximum extent permitted by applicable law. To the extent any element of the design does not qualify as a work for hire, Creator irrevocably assigns to Hood'd all right, title, and interest (including all copyrights, trademarks, and moral rights, waived to the fullest extent permitted) in and to the final design, all iterations, sketches, references, and derivatives thereof. Creator shall execute any documents reasonably required to perfect this assignment.

6.2 **Creator third-party clearance.** Creator represents that any inputs, references, or contributions provided to Hood'd are either original to Creator or used with full written permission from the rights holder. Creator shall not provide any element that references or incorporates a third party's trademark, likeness, or copyrighted work without documented written clearance.

6.3 **Creator promotional content license.** Creator retains ownership of promotional content they produce about the Drop Product. Creator grants Hood'd a perpetual, worldwide, royalty-free, sublicensable, transferable license to reproduce, display, distribute, adapt, and remix that content in Hood'd's marketing, advertising, social, email, website, and sales enablement, including paid media. This license survives termination.

6.4 **Hood'd brand.** Creator receives no rights to Hood'd trademarks, brand assets, or other Hood'd IP beyond what is strictly necessary to execute Creator's deliverables under this agreement. No merchandising rights.

## 7. Representations and Restrictions

Creator represents that:

1. They have not granted exclusive representation or product-endorsement rights to any agent, manager, label, or platform that would conflict with this agreement.
2. **They are at least 18 years of age.** This agreement cannot be executed by or on behalf of a minor.
3. No aspect of the collaboration will include or reference any third party's trademark, likeness, copyrighted work, or protected IP without that party's written permission.
4. The statements Creator makes publicly about Hood'd and the Drop Product will be truthful and substantiated.

Creator agrees NOT to:

1. Publicly disclose the Drop Product design, pricing, or launch date before drop launch.
2. Sell, transfer, or license the Drop Product design to any third party.
3. **Produce, promote, or endorse any competing automotive hood cover featuring the same nation theme and substantially similar visual identity for a period of thirty (30) days after drop close.** This restriction is limited to the specific product category (automotive hood covers) and specific theme (the nation and visual identity of the Drop Product). It does not restrict Creator from collaborating with other apparel, accessory, or automotive brands in any other category.
4. Make claims about Hood'd, its products, or its business that are materially inaccurate or disparaging.

## 8. Kill Clause and Definitions

8.1 **Design finalization.** "Final design approval" means Creator has provided written confirmation (email, text, or e-signature) that the current design file is approved for print.

8.2 **Material failure.** A party "materially fails" when (a) they fail to deliver a required deliverable by the agreed date and fail to cure within 72 hours of written notice, or (b) they fail to respond to reasonable communication for 7 consecutive calendar days.

8.3 **Hood'd termination.** Hood'd may terminate before final design approval if Creator materially fails. Upon termination, Hood'd may complete the design independently and proceed without Creator; Creator forfeits revenue share for that drop but retains rights to promotional content already posted.

8.4 **Creator termination.** Creator may terminate before final design approval with written notice; no fault or penalty.

8.5 **After final design approval.** Neither party may withdraw, except for material breach that is not cured within 10 calendar days of written notice.

## 9. Confidentiality

Both parties shall hold confidential information received from the other in confidence, including design iterations, sales figures, pricing logic, customer data, and business strategy. Obligation survives **24 months** after drop close. Standard carveouts (publicly available information, independently developed, required by law with prompt notice) apply.

## 10. Liability

10.1 **General cap.** Except as provided in Section 10.2, neither party's aggregate liability to the other under this agreement shall exceed the greater of (a) total revenue share paid or payable under this agreement, or (b) US$1,000. Neither party is liable for indirect, consequential, special, incidental, or punitive damages.

10.2 **Carveouts.** The limitations in Section 10.1 do NOT apply to:
(a) a party's indemnification obligations under Section 18;
(b) breach of confidentiality under Section 9;
(c) breach of intellectual property obligations under Section 6 or representations under Section 7;
(d) willful misconduct, gross negligence, or fraud; or
(e) Creator's obligation to refund overpayments under Section 5.3.

## 11. Governing Law and Dispute Resolution

11.1 **Governing law.** This agreement is governed by the laws of the **Province of Ontario, Canada** and the federal laws of Canada applicable therein, without regard to conflict-of-laws principles.

11.2 **Negotiation first.** Any dispute arising out of or relating to this agreement shall first be addressed through good-faith negotiation between the parties for a period of 15 business days.

11.3 **Binding arbitration.** If negotiation fails, the dispute shall be resolved by binding arbitration administered by the **ADR Institute of Canada (ADRIC)** under its Arbitration Rules, by a single arbitrator, seated in **Toronto, Ontario**, conducted in English. Judgment on the award may be entered in any court of competent jurisdiction.

11.4 **Class action waiver.** The parties waive any right to participate in a class, collective, or representative action in connection with this agreement. Disputes shall be brought in the party's individual capacity only.

11.5 **Injunctive relief exception.** Either party may seek injunctive or equitable relief in a court of competent jurisdiction to protect intellectual property or confidential information, without waiving arbitration rights on monetary claims.

## 12. Entire Agreement

This document is the entire agreement between the parties with respect to the Drop Product and supersedes all prior discussions. Modifications must be in writing and signed by both parties.

## 13. Force Majeure

Neither party is liable for failure to perform caused by events beyond reasonable control, including natural disasters, acts of war or terrorism, pandemics, government action, platform outages (Shopify, Stripe, fulfillment partner), or shipping-carrier disruption. The affected party shall give prompt notice and use reasonable efforts to mitigate. If a force majeure event prevents performance for more than 30 days, either party may terminate without liability beyond amounts already earned.

## 14. Notices

All formal notices must be in writing and sent to the email addresses in the signature block. Notices are deemed delivered on the next business day after transmission, provided no bounce or failure response is received. Either party may update their notice address with 5 business days' written notice.

## 15. Assignment

Neither party may assign this agreement without the other party's written consent, except Hood'd may assign to a successor in connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of substantially all assets, provided the assignee assumes all obligations.

## 16. Severability

If any provision is found unenforceable, the remainder of this agreement remains in effect and the unenforceable provision shall be reformed to the minimum extent necessary to be enforceable while preserving the parties' intent.

## 17. Electronic Signature

This agreement may be executed electronically and in counterparts. Electronic signatures have the same legal effect as original wet signatures under the Ontario Electronic Commerce Act, 2000 and the U.S. ESIGN Act.

## 18. Mutual Indemnification

18.1 **Creator indemnifies Hood'd** against third-party claims arising from (a) Creator's breach of Sections 6.2 or 7, (b) Creator's promotional content (other than content Hood'd edits or remixes), or (c) Creator's breach of endorsement disclosure obligations under Section 2.6.

18.2 **Hood'd indemnifies Creator** against third-party claims arising from (a) the Drop Product itself (product liability, defects, injury), (b) Hood'd's use of Creator's promotional content in Hood'd marketing beyond the license granted, or (c) Hood'd's breach of its representations under this agreement.

18.3 **Process.** The indemnified party shall give prompt notice, allow the indemnifying party to control defense and settlement (provided no settlement admits liability or imposes obligations on the indemnified party without consent), and provide reasonable cooperation at the indemnifying party's expense.
