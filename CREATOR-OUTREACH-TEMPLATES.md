# Creator Outreach Templates — v1

**For:** Dan / CEO session to send directly or adapt
**Tone:** Direct, professional, no gushing, no hype. Respect their time.
**Goal:** Get a reply, not close the deal in one message.

Every template should be personalized before sending. Creators can smell a mass-blast in one line.

---

## Template A — Cold DM (Instagram / TikTok)

**When to use:** First contact via the creator's primary channel. Keep under 400 characters if possible.

```
Hey [first name] — Dan, founder of Hood'd Shop (hooddshop.com).
We make stretch-fit car hood covers for World Cup 2026 nations.

Building a limited-drop series with creators — co-designed, 48hr window, you get a rev share on every unit sold. No upfront cost to you, no exclusivity.

Your [specific thing — e.g., "Mexico content" / "car build series"] is the reason I'm reaching out. Worth a quick DM to explore?
```

**Customization points:**
- `[first name]` — use their real name if known, handle only if not
- `[specific thing]` — one concrete reference to their recent content. If you can't name one, they're not worth outreach.

---

## Template B — Cold Email

**When to use:** When you have an email address. Slightly longer format acceptable.
**Subject line options (pick one):**
- `Quick collab idea — Hood'd × [Handle]`
- `Limited drop collab — rev share, no upfront`
- `Hood'd is building a creator drop series — interested?`

```
Hey [first name],

I'm Dan, founder of Hood'd Shop (hooddshop.com). We make sublimation-printed car hood covers for all 48 World Cup 2026 nations — premium, stretch-fit, $49.99 standard line.

Over the next 12 weeks leading into the tournament, we're running a limited-edition drop series. Selected creators co-design one piece, we handle production and fulfillment, drops run for 48 hours at $64.99, and you earn $7 on every unit sold.

No upfront fee, no exclusivity outside the 48hr drop window, no long commitment. One drop, one agreement, one payment.

What pulled me to you: [specific, concrete, 1–2 sentences about their work]

If this is interesting I can send a one-page agreement and a concept brief. If it's not the right fit, no worries — thanks for reading this far.

Dan Fruman
Founder, Hood'd Shop
hooddshop.com
[email]
```

## Template C — Referral Intro

**When to use:** An existing collaborator introduces you to another creator.
**Format:** Reply directly to the intro thread.

```
[Referrer name] — thanks for the intro, appreciate it.

[Creator first name] — good to meet you. Quick context: Hood'd makes car hood covers for World Cup 2026, and we're running a limited-drop collab series. [Referrer] and I [are working on / worked on] a drop together.

Same structure for every creator: you co-design one piece, 48hr drop window on our site, $7/unit rev share, no upfront fee. I can send the one-pager if you want to take a look.

Open to a quick DM exchange if this is interesting.
```

**Referral bonus to note internally:** If a referral converts to a signed collab, offer the referrer a $100 flat bonus on top of their own rev share. Not obligatory, not promised publicly, just good practice.

## Template D — Follow-Up 1 (Day 4 after initial)

**When to use:** No response to Template A or B after 3–4 days.

```
Hey [first name] — following up once in case the last message got buried.

TL;DR: limited-edition car hood cover drop, you co-design one piece, 48hr window, $7/unit rev share on every sale. No upfront, no exclusivity.

Happy to share the one-page agreement so you can judge the terms directly. If it's not a fit now, totally fine — I'll stop there.
```

## Template E — Follow-Up 2 (Day 10 after initial)

**When to use:** Single last attempt. After this, mark the creator "no response" and move on.

If timing is off now, keep me in mind — we're running drops from now through the World Cup final in July. Happy to circle back when the calendar makes sense.

## Template F — Warm "You're Selected" Brief

**When to use:** Creator has replied positively and passed the five-filter screen. This is the first formal message laying out the opportunity.

```
[First name] —

Great to hear back. Here's the full brief so you can make a clean decision:

WHAT WE'D BUILD TOGETHER
— One limited-edition Hood'd car hood cover, co-designed with you
— Themed around [proposed nation / theme], open to your input
— 48-hour drop window on hooddshop.com
— Retail price: $[64.99 or 69.99]

YOUR ROLE
— 30-min async or live design kickoff: colors, symbols, mood, references
— Up to 3 rounds of feedback on our concepts
— Sign off on final design
— Post a teaser (T-3 days), a launch post (T-0), and a recap (T-post drop)
— Keep the design confidential until launch

OUR ROLE
— Produce and refine the design based on your input
— Manufacturing, inventory, shipping, customer service — all on us
— Two free units shipped to you
— Post-drop settlement within 14 days of close

YOUR CUT
— $7 USD per unit sold in the drop window
— Typical drop target: 50–100 units → $350–$700 per drop
— Strong drops: 200+ units → $1,400+
— Paid via [Stripe Connect / PayPal — your preference]

TERMS
— One drop, one agreement. No long commitment.
— We own the final design IP. You keep your promotional content.
— 90-day non-compete on substantially similar products after drop close.

Next step if you're in: I send a one-page agreement for e-sign, and we schedule the design kickoff for next week. Typical timeline is 14 days from kickoff to drop day.

Let me know and I'll send the paperwork.
```

## Template G — Pass / Decline (Creator-Initiated)

**When to use:** Creator declines. Always reply. Leave the door open.

```
Got it [first name] — appreciate you getting back to me either way.

If the timing changes or the structure would work differently for you, door's open. Best of luck with [recent project].
```

## Template H — Pass / Decline (Hood'd-Initiated)

**When to use:** Creator expressed interest but failed the five-filter screen (low engagement, no car, taste mismatch, etc.). Be polite and non-specific.

```
[First name] — thanks for the back-and-forth.

After looking at the overall fit for this drop series, we're going to pass this round. Nothing against your work — just looking for specific overlap with our product story. Wishing you well with it.
```

## Template I — Post-Drop Thank You + Future Hint

**When to use:** After a successful first drop, to leave space for a second collaboration later.

```

— Units sold: [X]
— Revenue: $[X]
— Your cut: $[X], processing to [method] now
— Timeline: paid within 14 days per the agreement

Full settlement PDF attached. Thanks for the work on this one — [specific honest compliment about what worked, e.g., "the launch post crushed, highest first-hour conversion we've seen"].

We'll be doing more drops through the tournament window. If you're up for a second one, let's talk mid-[month].
```

## DO NOT Send

- Messages with emoji in the subject line or greeting
- Messages claiming "huge opportunity" / "once in a lifetime" / "massive"
- Messages that don't name the creator specifically
- Messages with typos in the creator's handle or name (instant disqualification on their side)
- Messages that mention revenue targets you can't back up
- Messages that imply exclusivity without offering it
- Messages that copy a big-brand tone (Hood'd is a small founder-led shop, lean into that)

---

## Response-Rate Benchmarks

Based on general creator-outreach data, not Hood'd-specific yet:

- **Cold DM:** 10–20% reply rate expected
- **Cold Email:** 5–15% reply rate expected
- **Referral intro:** 40–60% reply rate expected
- **Follow-up 1:** adds 5–8% recovery
- **Follow-up 2:** adds 2–3% recovery

If Hood'd is tracking below 8% on cold outreach after 20 sent, something in the targeting or the pitch is off. Stop and review before continuing to burn through the list.

## Tracking

Every outreach should be logged in the CRM with:
- Creator name + handle
- Template used
- Date sent
- Personalization angle
- Response (yes / no / no response / not yet)
- Date of response
- Follow-up status
- Outcome (contracted / declined / ghosted)

Review cohort data weekly.
